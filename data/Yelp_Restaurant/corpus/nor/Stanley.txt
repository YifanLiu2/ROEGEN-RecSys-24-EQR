Bananas Foster French Toast ($12.00)- Bananas Foster French Toast is extremely popular in New Orleans because it was created there! So I made it a mission to order it at every brunch spot I visited and Stanley's took the crown as being the best tasting.  While it's extremely sweet and serves more as dessert, I loved the feathery feel of the french toast.  It's generously sprinkled with cinnamon, sliced bananas, toasted walnuts, Foster Sauce, and a scoop of Vanilla Ice Cream.  I suggest sharing this meal with someone as it's rather large and could be overly sweet for most.
Louisiana Jambalaya ($8.50)- Appealing to the senses, this plate is beautifully made with spicy chicken and andouille sausage.  I do wish it was more spicy, but the smokey, tomato based, rice shined throughout the meal.  By far the best Jambalaya I had.  My only regret is not ordering a large size that can be had for $12.50.

About my ratings:
Only fine dining establishments can garner five stars.  Until yelp realizes that a fast food joint cannot possibly be ranked equally with a $100 per person place, I need to draw the line somewhere.  If we were only talking about the quality and presentation of the food, it would be a different story.  But, as we know, yelp's rankings take into account the entire dining experience.
Therefore, I divide restaurants into three categories (and I wish yelp would do the same):
1) fast food (no table service)
2) casual dining (table service)
3) fine dining (linen napkins, no plastic cups, no TV's in dining room, typically more expensive than casual dining)
Fine dining can earn a max of five stars.  Casual dining can earn a max of four stars.  Fast food can earn a max of three stars.This was about 930am on the first day of French Quarter festival.
